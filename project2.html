Analysis of Data Science Job Market from 2020 to 2023

The Average Remote Ratio by Experience Levels and Employment Modes

The charts below illustrate the average remote ratio across various experience levels and employment modes. These interactive charts dynamically update based on user selections.

For example, by clicking on "Part Time" in the first chart, all the part-time jobs will be selected in the second chart as well.

This project aims to assist aspiring data science enthusiasts in their job search by identifying high-paying roles that offer favorable remote work benefits. The second chart provides an overview of all the jobs in the market, plotted based on salary and remote ratio.

  • The charts are designed to facilitate the job search process for data science enthusiasts.
  • Remote work benefits are a crucial factor in the selection of high-paying roles.
  • Both charts dynamically update in response to user interactions.
  • The second chart offers a comprehensive view of the job market, considering salary and remote work options.

Salary, Company Size, and Employment Mode

The following charts represent the salary of a role along with company size and mode of employment. When selecting "Entry Level" in the first chart, you can view the top Data Science jobs tagged as "Entry Level." The first chart illustrates the average salary versus experience level across various company sizes. Here are some insights we can gather from this:

  • Entry Level employees receive the highest salaries in medium-sized companies.
  • Executive Level employees earn the highest salaries in small startups.
  • The highest-paid entry-level position is the Machine Learning Scientist role.
  • The overall highest-paid entry-level position is the Data Science Tech Lead role.
  • Executive Level employees receive higher pay in contract jobs compared to full-time positions.

Data Analysis for the Time Period of 2020 to 2023

The following charts analyze the data over the time period of 2020 to 2023. Salary trends have consistently increased for all experience levels during these three years. However, senior-level employees experienced a drop in salaries in the year 2021, which can be attributed to the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic.

On the other hand, the average remote ratio has shown a decline with its peak occurring in 2021-2022. Large and medium-sized companies have witnessed the most significant drop in remote ratio compared to small startups.

On the left-hand side, we can observe what I believe are the best roles. These roles offer salaries above $100,000 and have the highest remote ratio.
